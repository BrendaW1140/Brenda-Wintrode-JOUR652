By Brenda Wintrode

In the 2018 elections, 40 candidates spent over $4.4 million dollars on 20 seat races to represent Anne Arundel County in the Maryland General Assembly and all of the districts but one remained in the hands of the same party, according to an analysis of their campaign finance reports.

The spending anomaly was created by the budget of Speaker of the House Michael E. Busch, a popular decades-long staple in Annapolis politics. Busch, who passed away in April, transferred close to $1 million to the House Democratic Caucus Committee, slates or other candidates’ campaigns.

Brooks Schandelmeier, treasurer for the Anne Arundel Democratic Central Committee, said that it’s typical for the leaders of both parties to raise money for their respective house and senate committees.

“What you do as a leader is you raise a bunch of money, and you give it to the house caucus or you give it directly to other campaigns,” Schandelmeier said, who is also the president of the democratic club in Busch’s former district.

Schandelmeier said, that for the Democrats, these funds are used strategically to support vulnerable Democrats or challenge a vulnerable Republican seat.

“Certainly an incumbent can raise money more than a candidate because they’re already there. And the people that are trying to influence those elected officials will give more money,” said chairman of the Anne Arundel Republican Central Committee and treasurer for several political campaigns.

Del. Nic Kipke (R-Pasadena), house minority leader, was a similar breadwinner for the Republicans, according to finance reports.
